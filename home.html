Bronx Zoo Presents: Name a Roach

Just ended things with your sweetheart? Not a big fan of Valentine's Day? Seeking some sweet revenge on the last person who broke your heart?
Thanks to the tireless staff of Bronx Zoo, this Valentine's Day, you can name a cockroach after an ex. The roaches in question are called Madagascar hissing cockroaches. For a donation of only $15, the zoo will print an official certificate with the name of your cockroach and then feed the roach to a zoo animal on February 14. No need to stress—you won't face reproach.



Don't live near the Bronx? No problem. Donate online or consider making a donation to one of many zoos across the United States participating in this special Valentine's Day tradition. Each zoo has put its own special flair on this unique Valentine's Day gift. The San Antonio Zoo is hosting its inagural "Cry me a Cockroach" event during which patrons can watch the cockroaches they've named be fed to various animals. Other zoos have diversified their offerings, such as San Francisco Zoo also encouraging scorned lovers to adopt a scorpion for an even nastier sting.


Information about Bronx Zoo from the Wildlife Conservation Society's website:

The Bronx Zoo, located on 265 acres of hardwood forest in Bronx, NY, opened on Nov. 8, 1899. It is world-renowned for its leadership in the areas of animal welfare, husbandry, veterinary care, education, science and conservation. The zoo is accredited by the Association of Zoos and Aquariums (AZA) and is the flagship park of the Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS) which manages the world’s largest network of urban wildlife parks including the Bronx Zoo, Central Park Zoo, Prospect Park Zoo, Queens Zoo and New York Aquarium. Our curators and animal care staff work to save, propagate, and sustain populations of threatened and endangered species. We have educated and inspired more than 400 million visitors at our zoos and aquarium since our opening and host approximately 4 million guests at our parks each year – including about a half-million students annually. The Bronx Zoo is the largest youth employer in the borough of the Bronx, providing opportunity and helping to transform lives in one of the most under-served communities in the nation. The Bronx Zoo is the subject of THE ZOO, a docu-series aired world-wide on Animal Planet.
